The Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development is an intergovernmental economic organisation with 35 member countries, founded in 1960 to stimulate economic progress and world trade. The data in this visualization was gathered from the OECD stats page.

<
Household Income This is the gross annual income (including public entitlement distributions) per household, minus taxes, social security contributions and depreciation of capital goods.
Household Net Wealth This is all currency, securities, loans, equity, insurance technical reserves, and other accounts receivable or payable per household, net of financial liabilities.
Unemployment Rate This is percent of the labor force unemployed for one year or more. Unemployed persons are defined as those who are currently not working but are willing to do so and actively searching for work.
Personal Earnings This is the average annual wages per full-time equivalent dependent employee. It considers the employees' total pay before any deductions are made by the employer.
Social Network Support This is is the percentage of respondents who respond positively when asked: “If you were in trouble, do you have relatives or friends you can count on to help you whenever you need them, or not?”.
Educational Attainment This is the number of adults aged 25 to 64 holding at least an upper secondary degree over the population of the same age, as defined by the OECD-ISCED classification.
Years In Education This is the average duration of education in which a 5 year old child can expect to enroll during his/her lifetime until the age of 39.
Air Pollution This is the population weighted average of annual concentrations of particulate matters less than 2.5 microns in diameter (PM2.5) in the air.
Water Quality This is the percentage of people who responded they are satisfied when asked: "In the city or area where you live, are you satisfied or dissatisfied with the quality of water?". .
Voter Turnout This is the ratio between the number of individuals who cast a ballot during an election and the total population registered to vote. It considers the elections that have attracted the largest number of voters in each country.
Life Expectancy This is how long, on average, people can expect to live based on the age-specific death rates currently prevailing. This measure refers to people born today and is computed as a weighted average of life expectancy for men and women.
Self-reported Health This is the percentage of the population aged 15 years old and over who report “good” or better health when responding to the question “How is your health in general?”.
Life Satisfaction This is a weighted-sum of different response categories based on people's rates of their current life relative to the best and worst possible lives for them on a scale from 0 to 10.
Homicide Rate This is the number of murders per 100,000 inhabitants over one year. It's not fully representative of safety but it is a reliable measure because, unlike other crimes, murders are almost always reported to the police.
Time Devoted To Leisure This is the amount of minutes (or hours) per day that, on average, full-time employed people spend on leisure and on personal care activities.
