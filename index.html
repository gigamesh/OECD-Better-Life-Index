LIFE INDEX

The Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development is an intergovernmental economic organisation with 35 member countries, founded in 1960 to stimulate economic progress and world trade. The data in this visualization was gathered from the OECD stats page.

<
Household Income The maximum amount that a household can afford to consume without having to reduce its assets or to increase its liabilities.
Household Net Wealth Currency, securities, loans, shares and other equity, insurance technical reserves, and other accounts receivable or payable, net of household financial liabilities.
Unemployment Rate The percent of the labor force unemployed for one year or more. Unemployed persons are defined as those who are currently not working but are willing to do so and actively searching for work.
Personal Earnings The average annual wages per full-time equivalent dependent employee. It considers the employees' total pay before any deductions are made by the employer.
Social Network Support This indicator is based on the question: “If you were in trouble, do you have relatives or friends you can count on to help you whenever you need them, or not?” and it considers the respondents who respond positively.
Educational Attainment Educational attainment considers the number of adults aged 25 to 64 holding at least an upper secondary degree over the population of the same age, as defined by the OECD-ISCED classification.
Years In Education This indicator is the average duration of education in which a 5 year old child can expect to enrol during his/her lifetime until the age of 39.
Air Pollution Air Pollution: The indicator is the population weighted average of annual concentrations of particulate matters less than 2.5 microns in diameter (PM2.5) in the air.
Water Quality This indicator is based on the question: "In the city or area where you live, are you satisfied or dissatisfied with the quality of water?". It's the percentage of people who responded they are satisfied.
Voter Turnout Voter turnout is defined as the ratio between the number of individuals that cast a ballot during an election to the population registered to vote. The indicator refers to the elections that have attracted the largest number of voters in each country.
Life Expectancy Life expectancy measures how long on average people could expect to live based on the age-specific death rates currently prevailing. This measure refers to people born today and is computed as a weighted average of life expectancy for men and women.
Self-reported Health This indicator refers to the percentage of the population aged 15 years old and over who report “good” or better health. The WHO recommends using a standard health interview survey to measure it, phrasing the question as “How is your health in general?”.
Life Satisfaction This indicator considers people's evaluation of their life as a whole. It is a weighted-sum of different response categories based on people's rates of their current life relative to the best and worst possible lives for them on a scale from 0 to 10.
Homicide Rate The number of murders per 100,000 inhabitants. It's not fully representative of safety but it is a reliable measure because, unlike other crimes, murders are usually reported to the police.
Time Devoted To Leisure Time Devoted To Leisure: This indicator measures the amount of minutes (or hours) per day that, on average, full-time employed people spend on leisure and on personal care activities.
